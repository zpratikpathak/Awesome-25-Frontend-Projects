Chapter IV

The Alchemist's
Shadow

It was during the late hours of a rain-soaked Tuesday that the library revealed its greatest secret. The dust motes danced in the candlelight, illuminated by a flame that refused to flicker despite the draft from the cracked stained-glass window.

Elias reached for the leather-bound volume, its spine cracked and weathered by centuries of neglect. He could feel the pulse of the parchment beneath his fingertips—a slow, rhythmic thrumming that echoed the beating of his own heart.

"Knowledge is not found in the clarity of the light, but in the depths of the shadows that it casts."

The ink seemed to shift as he watched, the letters crawling across the page like insects searching for a way out. He was not just reading history; he was witnessing the unraveling of time itself.
